Curriculum Vitae

Computational Chemistry
at the Frontier of Polarizability

I am a computational chemist who recently completed a PostDoctoral Fellowship (2024–2025) at the Henry Eyring Centre for Theoretical Chemistry, University of Utah, where I focused on modeling the dissolution and nucleation pathways of aluminate minerals in highly concentrated aqueous electrolytes.

Prior to this, I was a doctoral student at the Department of Chemistry, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Gandhinagar under Prof. Sairam S. Mallajosyula. My PhD research centered on computational chemistry and molecular modeling — specifically the interactions between nucleobases and graphene, and the effects of ions at the graphene–electrolyte interface.

I completed my Integrated M.Sc. program in Chemistry at Pondicherry University in 2018, and successfully defended my PhD Thesis on April 10, 2024.

Alongside my research, I develop scientific software — most notably Planck, a from-scratch quantum chemistry program implementing RHF, UHF, Kohn-Sham DFT, CASSCF, and more, written in modern C++23.

Research

Areas of Investigation

Bridging the gap between experimental observations and molecular-level understanding using polarizable force fields and ab initio methods.

⬡

Nucleobase Self-Assembly on Graphene

Employing Drude Polarizable FF simulations to demonstrate that canonical nucleobases form ordered self-assemblies over graphene surfaces — consistent with experimental observations that classical non-polarizable additive FFs fail to capture.

⚡

Graphene–Electrolyte Interface

Investigating how molecular polarizability governs ion–graphene binding energetics and water dipole moments in hydration shells of mono- and divalent ions, providing a scalable alternative to AIMD for interface studies.

⬡

DNA Bio-Nanotechnology

Probing ssDNA translocation dynamics through pristine graphene nanopores with Drude Polarizable FF simulations, revealing the role of π-stacking and hydrogen bonding in pore-clogging and pore-diameter-independent dynamics.

◎

Aluminate Mineral Dissolution

PostDoc research at the University of Utah (2024–2025): modeling dissolution and nucleation pathways of aluminate minerals in highly concentrated aqueous electrolytes — with implications for carbon capture and materials design.

Thesis

PhD Thesis Summary

My thesis investigated the effect of molecular polarizability on the dynamics of ssDNA through a graphene nanopore. Using the Drude polarizable force field as the primary computational framework, the work progressively builds from fundamental graphene–molecule interactions to biologically relevant translocation processes.

A key motivation was the significant discrepancy between experimental studies and MD simulations based on classical non-polarizable additive force fields. By incorporating atomic polarizability, we captured phenomena — self-assembly, electrolyte behavior, pore clogging — that eluded conventional approaches entirely.

Translocation of four ssDNA homopolymers through a polarizable graphene nanopore; pore clogging mechanisms and diameter-independent dynamics.

Coding Projects

Open-Source Scientific Software

Building quantum chemistry tools from first principles — combining deep theoretical understanding with modern software engineering.

Featured Project · C++23
Planck

A quantum chemistry program implementing RHF, UHF, and Kohn-Sham DFT with Obara-Saika integrals, analytic gradients, geometry optimization, vibrational analysis, multipole moments, symmetry detection, and binary checkpointing.

C++23 CMake Eigen 3.4 libxc libmsym OpenMP Python

RHF / UHF / RKS / UKS — Closed and open-shell Hartree-Fock and Kohn-Sham DFT via two dedicated executables

Dual integral engines — Obara-Saika for low angular momentum; Rys quadrature for high angular momentum with auto-selection

Post-HF methods — RMP2 / UMP2, CASSCF, SA-CASSCF, and RASSCF with active-space integral caching

Analytic nuclear gradients — RHF and UHF analytic gradients; geometry optimization in Cartesian and generalized internal coordinates

Vibrational analysis — Semi-numerical Hessian from finite differences; mass-weighted normal modes, frequencies in cm⁻¹, ZPE

Symmetry detection — Point group via libmsym; MO irreducible representation labels; Abelian-subgroup handling for non-Abelian groups

DIIS convergence — Automatic subspace restart; virtual orbital level-shifting for open-shell systems

Checkpoint system — Binary .hfchk files; same-basis restart and cross-basis density projection

Extensive basis set library — STO-nG, Pople split-valence, triple-zeta, Dunning cc, aug-cc; auto-fetched via Basis Set Exchange

Electric multipoles — Dipole and quadrupole moment analysis automatically after each SCF convergence
