Hydrogen bonding in Adenine nucleobases

Ph.D. Thesis

The overarching theme of my Ph.D. thesis is to develop and benchmark (Additive and Drude Polarizable) FF parameters that conform to CHARMM parameter set. We then employ such parameters to study the dynamics of various bio-molecules, to infer data about their physio-chemical properties. My first project aims at studying the effect of polarization on the aggregation of various molecules on nanosurfaces. We are also interested in studying the physio-chemical properties and the electronic responses on nanosurfaces upon interactions with moleules.
