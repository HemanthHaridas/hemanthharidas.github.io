Hello!

I am Hemanth Haridas, a Doctoral Student at Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar. I have been working with Dr. Sairam S. Mallajosyula since 2018. My research focuses on computational chemistry and molecular modeling, with an emphasis on the interactions between nucleobases and graphene. I use polarizable force field MD simulations to accurately capture the spontaneous self-assembly of higher-order structures in cytosine nucleobases and to study the effects of ions at the graphene-electrolyte interface. Prior to joining IIT Gandhinagar, I completed a 5-year Integrated M.Sc. program in Chemistry at Pondicherry University in 2018.

Featured Research

1. Polarization Influences the Evolution of Nucleobase-Graphene Interactions
In this paper, we investigated the influence of polarization on the evolution of nucleobase-graphene interactions. We used a polarizable force field to accurately capture the spontaneous self-assembly of higher-order structures in cytosine nucleobases that cannot be captured by nonpolarizable force fields. Our results showed that polarization plays a crucial role in the evolution of nucleobase-graphene interactions and can significantly affect the formation and stability of higher-order structures. In contrast, additive force fields that do not include polarizability were unable to accurately capture these interactions. This highlights the importance of including polarizability in molecular modeling studies of nucleobase-graphene interactions.
2. Capturing Concentration-Induced Aggregation of Nucleobases on a Graphene Surface through Polarizable Force Field Simulations
In this paper, we demonstrated the importance of using a polarizable force field to accurately capture the spontaneous self-assembly of higher-order structures in cytosine nucleobases. Our findings showed a gradual transition from an ordered 2D network to a sizable disordered network of hydrogen-bonded structures as the concentration of nucleobases increased from 0.25 to 0.75 M. This work highlights the significance of using polarizable force fields in simulating the behavior of nucleobases on a graphene surface.
3. Capturing charge and size effects of ions at the graphene-electrolyte interface using polarizable force field simulations
In this paper, we investigated the impact of ion charge and size on their distribution and mobility at the graphene-electrolyte interface. Our results showed that ion behavior is significantly influenced by their charge and size, which can have important implications for the design and optimization of graphene-based materials for various applications, including energy storage, water purification, and sensing. Our study provides valuable insights into the behavior of ions at the graphene-electrolyte interface and highlights the importance of considering ion charge and size effects in simulations of this system.
