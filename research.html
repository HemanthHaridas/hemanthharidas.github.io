Representative generator for a C-N Topological Insulator

Research Interests
Currently, my major research interest is the development and benchmarking of FF parameters that conform to the CHARMM parameterization stratergy. We are working on the generation, testing and benchmarking of parameters that can accurately describe novel molecules that are biologically important, but presently absent in CHARMM FF space. I am also interested in development of methodologies that can employed to efficiently to ascertain the optical nature of molecules, without expensive QM calculations.
I am also interested in studying the relationship between aromaticity, anti-aromaticity and topological insulators. My M.Sc. thesis was based on such themes, where we were able to show that pure C-N 2D structures can host dirac cones on or near Fermi levels.
