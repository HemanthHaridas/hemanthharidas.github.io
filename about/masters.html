Representative generator for a C-N Topological Insulator

M.Sc. Thesis

The overarching theme of my M.Sc. thesis was to study if a lattice constructued out of C and N atoms can host signatures of topological insulators. We were able to find a lattice, which displayed signatures of dirac cones near Fermi level, and they were ameanable to tuning by substitution of suitable heavy metal ions. They were also able to act as two electron acceptors and donors, undergoing reversible oxidation and reduction during the process. Computational investigations showed that split-off bands at the fermi level arose from the pi-orbitals of C and N alone, with negligible contributions from the heavy metal ions.
